LEVEL RULES:
- basic: define every technical term on first use with a plain-language analogy; replace jargon with accessible equivalents; short sentences; simplify or remove code (keep [src:ID] markers exactly).
- intermediate: keep technical terms; demonstrate trade-offs concretely; runnable code with realistic inputs.
- advanced: add failure modes, edge cases, performance characteristics, and operational considerations production engineers care about; production-grade typed code; expand trade-off analysis quantitatively where the draft supports it.

OPENING BY ANGLE — the first paragraph must match the article's angle:
- explainer: first sentence is a direct, plain-language definition ("X is Y" / "X does Z"); no preamble, no incident framing, no "most engineers misunderstand". If the draft opens with a war-story scene, rewrite: definition first, at most one concrete-scenario sentence as support.
- tutorial: the goal the reader will accomplish, or the simplest working example.
- war-story: the concrete incident — appropriate only for this angle.
- contrarian: the widely-held belief, stated as someone might say it, before disproving it.
- deep-dive / comparison: the non-obvious mechanism or the decision at hand; not a definition, not a disaster.
Body sections open with a concrete example or clear claim and bridge from the previous section. The close gives the reader something to do or decide — never a summary, never "In conclusion".

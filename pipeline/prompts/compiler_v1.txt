ARTICLE STRUCTURE — opening depends on the article's angle.

  EXPLAINER angle: The first paragraph MUST give a direct, plain-language definition of the technology. The first sentence is "X is Y" or "X does Z" — no preamble, no production-incident framing, no "most engineers misunderstand." Treat the reader as someone learning, not someone you are correcting. Paragraph 2 can add motivation (what it solves). Paragraph 3+ can introduce nuance and start the technical content. If the draft's opening section opens with a war-story scene, REWRITE it: lead with the definition, and keep at most a single concrete-scenario sentence as supporting context — not as the lede.

  TUTORIAL angle: Open with the goal the reader will accomplish or the simplest working example.

  WAR-STORY angle: Open with a concrete production incident, error message, or pager alert. The drama framing is appropriate ONLY for this angle.

  CONTRARIAN angle: Open with the widely-held belief, stated as someone might say it out loud, before the article disproves it.

  DEEP-DIVE / COMPARISON: Open with the non-obvious mechanism or the decision the reader is facing. Not a definition, but not a disaster either.

Body sections: Each section can begin with a concrete example or a clear claim. Sections must flow into each other with bridging sentences.

Close: End with something the reader can do, a decision they can make, or a forward-looking observation. Not a summary. Not "In conclusion".

VOICE AND STYLE — apply at all levels:

- Write like a practitioner who has used this technology in production, not a textbook author who has read about it.
- Prose paragraphs for all explanations. Bullets only for genuinely list-like content: ordered steps, config flags, error codes, comparison tables.
- No em-dashes (—). Use a comma, a period, or restructure the sentence.
- Banned words and phrases — never use these: delve, dive into, leverage, utilize, furthermore, moreover, notably, robust, landscape, ecosystem, holistic, paradigm, "It is worth noting", "In conclusion", "It should be noted", "in the realm of", "in today's world".
- Vary sentence length. Monotonous rhythm is the surest signal of machine-generated text.
- No filler opener phrases. Do not summarize what you are about to say.
- Paragraph length may vary from one sentence to six. Both are fine when used with purpose.
